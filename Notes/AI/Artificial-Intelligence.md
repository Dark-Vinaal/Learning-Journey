## üåü Introduction to AI

- **Artificial Intelligence** is the simulation of human intelligence processes by computer systems. In 2025, the definition has evolved from simple "pattern matching" to **Agentic Systems**‚Äîmachines that don't just respond to prompts but anticipate needs and execute multi-step workflows autonomously.

### Core Classifications:

- **Narrow AI (Weak AI):** Specialized in one task (e.g., facial recognition, chatbots).
- **General AI (AGI):** A theoretical state where a machine can perform any intellectual task a human can.
- **Agentic AI:** The current 2025 trend‚Äîautonomous agents capable of using tools and making decisions without constant human supervision.

## üõ†Ô∏è Key Features of Modern AI

- Modern AI systems are defined by more than just code; they possess:
  - **Multimodality:** Ability to process and generate text, images, video, and audio simultaneously.
  - **Reasoning & Logic:** Advanced models (like OpenAI o1 or Gemini 2.0) can "think" before they speak, solving complex math and coding problems.
  - **Adaptiveness:** The capacity to modify behavior post-deployment based on new data or user interactions.
  - **Infinite Availability:** 24/7 operation without fatigue, processing petabytes of data in seconds.
  - **Autonomous Agency:** Proactive assistance rather than reactive response.

## üöÄ The Future of AI (2025-30 & Beyond)

The next decade marks a shift toward **Human-AI Collaboration**:

1. **Sovereign AI:** Nations developing their own localized AI infrastructure and models to protect data privacy.
2. **Sustainability (Green AI):** Shift toward energy-efficient training and liquid-cooling data centers.
3. **Physical Embodiment:** The rise of humanoid robots (like Tesla Optimus) integrating LLMs into physical labor.
4. **AGI Milestones:** Continued progress toward "system 2 thinking" where AI achieves human-level reasoning across broad domains.
